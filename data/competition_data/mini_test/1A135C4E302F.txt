Phones and Driving

Everyday people die in car accidents because they use cell phones while driving. According to research cell phones are leading factors that cause accidents. Studies show that a cell phone put drivers at a four time greater risk of a crash. Despite the danger, people still use cell phones because they want to keep in touch with people. They also think that hands free is safe because they're driving with two hands.

Dangers of phones and driving

Fatal accidents related to cell phones and driving have risen dramatically over the years. It usually happens with people around 25 years or younger. 1 out of every 4 accidents in the U.S is caused by texting while driving. Answering a text while driving takes your attention away from the road for five seconds. If you're traveling 55 mph that's enough time to travel the length of a football field.

Recorded Statistics

Nearly 390,000 injuries accrue each year caused by texting while driving. You are six times more likely to cause an accident by texting while driving drunk. Texting is by far the most dangerous cell phone related task. 94% of drivers support a ban on texting while driving. 74% support a ban of handheld cell phone use.

Consequences of phones/driving

Georgia's driving law prevents text messaging and hand held cell phone use for all drivers. For drivers it is illegal to write, send, or read a text while driving.

The texting law applies to cell phones, tablets, messaging devices and laptops. First offense a motorist is looking at a fine for 50 dollars and a demerit. Second offense 100 dollar fine and two demerits. Thirds offense is 150 dollars and three demerits.
